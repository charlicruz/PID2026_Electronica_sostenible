Summary

What is the project about?

Context

Electronics is an essential technological foundation for communications, automation, computing, instrumentation, embedded intelligence and the Internet of Things. However, university teaching has traditionally focused on functional aspects, while paying less attention to the environmental impact associated with the life cycle of electronic systems.

The project responds to a context marked by the scarcity of strategic materials, the growth of electronic waste and the need to move towards more circular models.

Purpose

To design, implement and evaluate a common teaching activity on sustainable electronics, consisting of an explanatory session, a video or audiovisual resource, application examples and a questionnaire/quiz to collect evidence of student learning and perception.

Objectives

Technical competences and sustainability

01

Introduce sustainability applied to electronics

Life cycle, electronic waste, resource consumption, repair, reuse and platform upgrading.

02

Design transferable teaching resources

Presentation, video, short teacher guide, perception questionnaire and self-assessment quiz.

03

Present open and modular platforms

Examples such as GateMate FPGA, Arduino MKR Vidor 4000, Olimex STM32-H407 or STM32F407ZG.

04

Evaluate student impact

Voluntary questionnaires, qualitative analysis and evidence collected in courses or seminars.

Teaching contents

Main resources and concepts

Planetary boundaries and circular economy

From the linear “use and discard” model towards strategies based on repair, reuse and reduction of electronic waste.

Life Cycle Assessment

Assessment of environmental impact from raw material extraction to end of life using tools such as OpenLCA or Brightway2.

Open hardware and modularity

Use of open, modular and reconfigurable platforms to promote scalability, upgrading, repair and student autonomy.

Software eco-design

Event-driven design, energy efficiency, hardware abstraction layers and long-term maintainability.

Reuse and repair

Extending hardware lifetime, making use of existing platforms and reducing access barriers.

Critical materials and urban mining

Recovery of materials from electronic waste to reduce dependence on critical raw materials.

European connection

Inspired by the Erasmus+ DEEPCEL project

This initiative takes the DEEPCEL project as a reference. DEEPCEL aims to redefine the teaching of digital electronics through a paradigm based on green competences, life-cycle assessment, eco-design, modularity, reuse, upgrading and reconfigurability.
